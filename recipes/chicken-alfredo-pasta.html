Chicken Alfredo Pasta

Description

To make a delicious Chicken Alfredo pasta, start by cooking fettuccine noodles according to package instructions and set them aside. Meanwhile, season boneless, skinless chicken breasts with salt and pepper, then cook them in olive oil over medium heat until browned and cooked through. Remove the chicken from the pan and slice it into strips. In the same pan, melt butter and sauté minced garlic until fragrant. Stir in heavy cream, bringing it to a simmer before adding grated Parmesan cheese, stirring until smooth and creamy. Season the sauce with salt, pepper, and optional red pepper flakes for a bit of spice. Add the cooked fettuccine to the sauce and toss to coat. Serve the pasta topped with the sliced chicken and garnish with fresh parsley and extra Parmesan. Enjoy this creamy, flavorful dish with garlic bread or a side salad for a complete meal!

Ingredients

  • Fettuccine pasta
  • Chicken breasts
  • Olive Oil
  • Garlic
  • Heavy Cream
  • Butter
  • Parmesean Cheese
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Parsley
  • Red Pepper Flakes (Optional)

Steps

  1. Cook the fettuccine pasta according to package instructions, drain, and set aside.
  2. While the pasta cooks, season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat, then cook the chicken breasts until golden brown and cooked through, about 5–6 minutes per side.
  4. Remove the chicken from the pan and let it rest. Once cooled slightly, slice it into strips.
  5. In the same pan, melt the butter over medium heat, then sauté the minced garlic for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  6. Add the heavy cream and bring it to a simmer, allowing it to thicken slightly.
  7. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese until the sauce becomes smooth and creamy. Season with salt, pepper, and optional red pepper flakes.
  8. Add the cooked fettuccine to the sauce and toss to coat.
  9. Serve the pasta on plates, topping it with the sliced chicken.
  10. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley and more Parmesan if desired.
